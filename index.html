SOLAR IN AUSTRALIA

Per Capita Generation

Australia is a world leader in the adoption of solar. Particularly in the adoption of rooftop solar. In 2020 Australia ranked 1st in solar generation per person beating out world leaders such as Japan, China and the US.

Measuring Energy and Capacity

1 kW = 1,000 watts

1 MW = 1,000 kW

1 GW = 1,000 MW

1 kWh - Consuming or producing 1 kW of power for 1 hour

1 kW - The rate of consuming or producing 1,000 watts of power at any given moment.

Large Scale Solar Installations

PV systems with a capacity of more that 100 kilowatts are categorised as Large-Scale PV systems. This includes large rooftop or grounded systems and utility scale systems as well (systems that generates solar power to feed it into the grid). Australia is expected to continue expanding its utility-scale solar capacity, especially as energy storage technologies, like batteries, become more integrated into the grid, improving reliability.

Residential Install Capacity

PV systems with a capacity below 15kW are considered residential. Much of these systems are recognised as "rooftop solar". Rooftop solar now accounts for 11.2 per cent of Australia's electricity supply. The Australian government has provided significant financial incentives such as large rebates for homeowners to install rooftop solar systems, reducing upfront costs. As well as feed in tariffs that pay homeowners for extra generation that is fed into the grid.

Monthly PV Installation Capacity

The size of individual solar installations in Australia has increased over time. In the early stages, smaller systems of around 1.5 to 3 kW were common. However, as technology improved and energy needs grew, systems above 6 kW—sometimes even over 10 kW—have become more popular.

Utility-scale solar installations in Australia have also grown significantly in capacity over time. Early solar farms were relatively small, but recent projects are much larger, often exceeding 100 MW, with some surpassing 300 MW. This shift is driven by falling costs and government incentives.

Energy Generated From Renewables

Wind and solar energy have been the main contributors to more than doubling Australia’s renewable electricity generation over the past decade. The country’s renewable mix has become more diversified, driven by substantial growth in wind and solar capacity, while hydro’s contribution has gradually decreased.

Source

Impact On Wholesale Electricity Prices

The influence of solar energy on the electricity market is evident through its impact on wholesale prices. Solar panels generate power during the day, leading to a significant reduction in daytime electricity prices, with occasional negative pricing. This occurs because high solar output coincides with periods of lower demand, creating excess supply in the market and driving prices down.
